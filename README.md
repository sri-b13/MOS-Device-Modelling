# MOS-Device-Modelling
Breakdown of Square Law MOS Model vs BSIM Model Under Short Channel Effects


This project investigates the impact of MOSFET scaling on device behavior, with a focus on short-channel effects and the limitations of classical models. While the square-law model provides a useful foundation for long-channel devices, it fails to accurately capture phenomena such as threshold voltage roll-off, drain-induced barrier lowering (DIBL), mobility degradation, velocity saturation, and increased leakage currents that dominate at smaller channel lengths. Through MATLAB-based simulations, key characteristics of the MOSFET, and subthreshold behavior are analyzed to highlight these deviations. The results demonstrate reduced transconductance, degraded switching performance, and increased sensitivity to device scaling, emphasizing the importance of electrostatic control in modern transistors. This work bridges the gap between theoretical MOSFET models and practical device behavior, and underscores the need for advanced compact models, such as BSIM and physics-based models for emerging devices like double-gated MoS2 FETs, to accurately represent nanoscale device operation.
